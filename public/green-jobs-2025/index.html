About the Conference

The Green Jobs Career Fair & Conference is a premier event dedicated to sustainability, green career opportunities, and innovative solutions to environmental challenges. Organized by the Engineering Students Association (ESA) in collaboration with the Office of Career Services, this initiative aligns with the Chancellor‚Äôs Big 5 Agenda and aims to localize discussions from COP 29 (Conference of Parties) into grassroots efforts that drive real impact. This year‚Äôs conference will feature expert workshops, panel discussions, exhibitions, and project presentations focused on advancing sustainability.


Key topics include: Building the Capacity of Energy Prosumers, The Eastern Africa Power Pool, Sustainable Transportation, Opportunities for Mwananchi in Carbon Credits, Financing the Energy Transition Policy and Sustainable Building and Success Stories from Partners.

The event will also showcase e-mobility projects addressing real-world challenges (detailed in the link below). As a closing activity, we will host a tree-planting session along Ngong Road to contribute to reforestation efforts following the construction of the Nairobi Expressway. Join us in shaping the future of sustainability and green careers by turning global discussions into local action!

Meet Your Hosts

Chancellor's Agenda

Prof. Patrick Verkooijen is the current Chancellor of the University of Nairobi. He is the CEO of the Global Center on Adaptation, an international organization that works on climate change. The University of Nairobi's (UoN) Chancellor's agenda, known as the "Big 5," is a five-point plan to transform the university into a leader in education, technology, and entrepreneurship. Chancellor Patrick Verkooijen has a strong focus on promoting green jobs and sustainability at the University of Nairobi. He aims to equip students with the skills and knowledge needed for careers in renewable energy, conservation, and climate resilience. Through partnerships and initiatives, he seeks to create opportunities for graduates in these emerging fields, contributing to a greener economy in Kenya and beyond. Verkooijen's agenda also emphasizes research and innovation in green technologies and sustainable practices. By fostering a culture of environmental consciousness within the university, he encourages students and faculty to develop solutions for climate change and environmental challenges. This includes supporting projects that promote clean energy, waste management, and sustainable agriculture, ultimately contributing to a more sustainable future.

Vice Chancellor

Prof. Margaret J. Hutchinson is the Acting Vice Chancellor and Full Professor of Horticulture of the University of Nairobi. She is also the Substantive Deputy Vice Chancellor (Research, Innovation and Enterprise) and Immediate Acting Deputy Vice Chancellor (Finance, Planning and Development) at the University of Nairobi. Prof Hutchinson holds a PhD from the University of Guelph, Canada, and MSc and BSc from the University of Nairobi. She has over 43 years of experience in Agricultural research, extension, Innovation and Technology transfer and strategic administration. Prof Hutchinson consults with many organizations in matters Agriculture, Food and Nutrition Security, Gender Mainstreaming in Agriculture, Leadership, governance and Transformation nationally and internationally. She is a member of several professional bodies such as the International Society of Horticultural Science, African Crop Science Society, Horticultural Association of Kenya, Kenya Professional Association of Women in Agriculture and Environment, the Agricultural Society of Kenya and Institute of Directors. Prof Hutchinson is a highly motivated, self-driven, results-focused person who is passionate about economic, social and spiritual and societal transformation of individuals, persons, Country and the African Continent.

Deputy Vice Chancellor - Academics

Eng. Prof. A. N. Gitau is an Agricultural Engineer with over 30 years of academic and practical experience in Agricultural Mechanization and Infrastructure Development; Water Harvesting Technologies; Environment; Renewable Energies; Conservation Agriculture Technologies and Power and Machinery Engineering. He has held various positions including Lecturer and Head of Department at the Kenya Industrial Training Institute (KITI) and Kenya Methodist University. He was a full-time Lecturer, Senior Lecturer, and Associate Professor at the University of Nairobi. He served as Chairman of the Department of Environmental and Biosystems Engineering and acted as Dean of the School of Engineering. He is the current Deputy Vice Chancellor - Academics at the University of Nairobi and the current DVC of Academics. He is also a Council Member of the Institute of Engineers of Kenya (IEK).

About ESA

About ESA

The Engineering Students Association is a professional body of students from five departments in the school of engineering, University of Nairobi. The association is over 40 years old and has been instrumental in bridging the gap between academia and the engineering industry. The association endeavors to keep students informed about opportunities and trends in the engineering industry.

Rahma Anuro, Chair

Rahma is the chair of ESA. She is a visionary leader who is committed to empowering engineering students and shaping the future of the industry. Rahma is passionate about promoting innovation and collaboration among students, and she is dedicated to providing them with the resources and opportunities they need to succeed.

Caleb Oira, Vice Chair

Caleb is a dynamic and dedicated leader serving as the vice chair of the Engineering Students Association. He takes pride in being part of the team that organized the highly successful Green Conference. He believes his unique blend of energy and precision will be instrumental in elevating the ESA into a powerhouse for the benefit of all engineering students at the University of Nairobi.

Andrew Kiongo, Secretary General

Andrew is an engineering student passionate about bringing young minds together to explore practical solutions for a greener future. Through his work with ESA, he helps create spaces like The Green Jobs Career Fair and Conference, where students can connect with industry leaders, share ideas, and discover opportunities in the growing green economy.

Office of Career Services

The University of Nairobi's Office of Career Services plays a crucial role in preparing its students and graduates for the professional world. It acts as a vital link between academia and industry, offering a comprehensive suite of services designed to equip students with the skills and resources they need to succeed in their career journeys. UoN's Career Services aims to empower students to explore career paths, develop essential job-seeking skills, and connect with potential employers.
